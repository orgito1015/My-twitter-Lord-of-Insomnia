The 3 A.M. Feeling

There's a specific kind of quiet that only exists after midnight. Not peaceful — just empty. The world has fallen asleep and you're still sitting here, running through things you should've said, things you should've done differently, things you wish you could un-feel.

It's the hour when every thought becomes louder than it deserves. A text message you never sent replays itself. A conversation from three years ago suddenly needs a better ending. The ceiling offers no answers, but you keep looking anyway.

"The worst part isn't the sadness. It's how familiar it starts to feel — like coming home to a place you never wanted to live."

I've started writing things down at this hour. Not to fix them. Not to process them into something productive. Just to put them somewhere outside of my head, where they take up less space.

If you're reading this at 3 a.m. — you already know. You don't need me to explain it. Just know that somewhere, someone else is awake with you.

That has to count for something.
