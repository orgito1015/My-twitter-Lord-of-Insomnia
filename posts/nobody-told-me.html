Nobody Told Me It Would Feel Like This

I used to think sadness was something that happened to you in a dramatic moment — a door slamming, rain on a funeral, a big scene you could point to and say "that's when it started."

Turns out it mostly looks like staring at your phone waiting for a message that's not coming. It looks like making coffee and forgetting to drink it. It looks like laughing at something and then feeling a dull ache a second later, because you almost forgot — and then you remembered.

"Grief doesn't announce itself. It just quietly rearranges the furniture while you weren't looking, and suddenly nothing feels like it's in the right place."

Nobody warned me that some days would be fine. That's the part that catches you off guard. You'll have a perfectly normal afternoon and think maybe I'm okay now — and then 11 p.m. arrives and it's all back, unchanged, patient as ever.

I'm writing this because I couldn't explain it out loud. Words work differently when no one's watching.

If you recognize this feeling — keep going. Quietly. At your own pace.
