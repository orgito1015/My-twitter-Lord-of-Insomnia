That Song I Can't Listen to Anymore

You know the one.

It wasn't even your song together. It just happened to be playing at the exact right — or wrong — moment. A car ride. A late-night kitchen. A phone pressed against an ear in the dark. And now it belongs to that moment, sealed inside it like something in amber, and you can't get it back.

The algorithm plays it sometimes. Shuffle doesn't know. You reach for skip and miss by half a second, and then three notes in and it's already too late — you're back there again, completely.

"Music doesn't carry memories. It is the memory. The melody is just a door you didn't know was still unlocked."

I've been building a playlist of songs I can't touch. A graveyard of good moments that are now too heavy to hold.

Maybe one day I'll be able to listen again. Maybe one day the song will just be a song.

Not today, though.
